Matchmaking

 

Posted on 16th August, 2019

Arranged marriages have been a long-standing tradition in the Indian subcontinent, where families arrange the union of a bride and groom. With the dawn of the internet, these classifieds moved to the digital space. From catering to communities based on region, religion, and socio-economic standing, to even separate portals for people serving in the military, divorcees and Mangaliks matchmaking has become an industry.With a revenue of almost three billion Indian rupees in the financial year 2017, from one matchmaking online site, this matchmaking business is growing in demand in India.With about 74 percent of young adults in the South Asian country in favor of having arranged marriages and a population that annually spends billions on weddings, it seems like there is no slowing this engine down, which subsequently also boosts the e-commerce industry of India. 

Movies are a reflection of society but at the same time, we also expect movies to be made which can impact and may trigger the need for change in the society for the better. Though the show has been trolled in various social media platforms, it is recommended to just open your mind and thoughts as the show doesn’t actually praise itself in doing so, it brings out the raw truth of the society. It is more like a wake up call.The show is a lighter take on modern and affluent indian matchmaking and holds a mirror  to the unfortunate, extremely patriarchal Indian society. It  lays down the reality for many indians or indian originated living abroad families. It very intelligently portrays the dynamics of the family, their wishes and engagement in bride / groom selection procedure. Like how height of the groom can be a problem in some families as according to Indian culture, the groom must be taller than the bride. Some families are very caste specific, though it may seem wrong but it draws light upon the fact that things must change. This show highlights both the aristocrats and the middle or lower class families in the Indian society. The rich modern families let their kids find their own matches, while the middle class and the poor are conservative and tend to believe in astrology. At the end,The dilemma of every Indian parent or family to find the right match for their ward or guide them..

 

The matchmaking industry is almost two decades old in India and it has potentially increased choices for those seeking partners..People in India believe that finding a match via the matchmaking route gives them a higher chance to evaluate matches.The matchmaking revolution has evolved so much that that India today is considered one of the fastest growing market for online dating and matchmaking applications.
