Indian Automobile Industry

 

Posted on August, 2020

Introduction/Exposure about the industry

Indian Automobile Industry has witnessed growth after Independence before which it was only limited to working on the maintenance, servicing, and dealership of the imported vehicles. Post Independence, the industry experienced substantial growth with the beginning of production and manufacturing of vehicles in India. The Indian Automobile Industry comprises two-wheelers, cars, trucks, tractors, three-wheelers and buses that together contribute a valuable share in the Indian Economy.
Some of the major Indian Automobile Manufacturers are:

 
  • Bajaj Auto
  • Hyundai Motor India Ltd.
  • TVS Motors
  • Maruti Udyog Ltd.
  • Hero Motors
  • Ford India Ltd.
  • Royal Enfield Motors

The automobile industry in India has a great potential in terms of employment with its emerging growth rate to be 18% per annum.



Valuation about the industry

With the introduction of reforms like GST under which GST on electric vehicles was revised to 5 per cent which was 12 per cent earlier, it is estimated that the Indian Automobile Industry will experience an exponential growth in the coming years.
As per the Indian Automobile Industry report by IBEF, “Overall, automobile export reached 4.77 million vehicles in FY20, growing at a CAGR of 6.94 per cent during FY16-FY20. Two wheelers made up 73.9 per cent of the vehicles exported, followed by passenger vehicles at 14.2 per cent, three wheelers at 10.5 percent and commercial vehicles at 1.3 per cent.”. According to this statistics, it is clear that two wheelers along with passenger vehicles dominate the Indian Automobile sector. Also, there is a great opportunity for the electric and autonomous vehicles to capture the Indian automobile sector as “The industry has attracted Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) worth US$ 24.21 billion between April 2000 and March 2020, according to the data released by Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT)”.

Skills needed to get into the industry/How to approach these companies

Skill set required:

  • Hands-on experience with the simulation tools like Solidworks, AutoCAD, etc.
  • Good communication and problem solving skills
  • Theoretical and practical knowledge of the working of different parts in automobiles.
  • High order thinking skills
  • Good understanding of Mechanics, Mathematics and Electronics

How to Approach automobile companies

  • Apply directly through the company website.
  • Apply through job portals like Naukri.com, LinkedIn.com, Indeed.com, monsterindia.com, etc.


Latest Trends/News of that Industry

According to the ‘Future of Jobs in India: A 2022 perspective’ report which was launched by EY, the survey was commissioned by FICCI and NASSCOM, it says that around 60-65 percent of jobs in the Indian Automobile sector would require new skill sets by 2022. This is because the introduction of technologies like Iot, cloud computing, big data, and automated systems have made it easier to replace the manual assembly and production work to be done by automated Robots.
Latest Job roles in the automotive sector woud include:

  • Automobile analytics engineer
  • Machine learning based vehicle cyber security expert
  • 3D printing technician
  • Sustainability integration expert.

There is a need to start creating collaborative learning platforms for the aspirants who want to get skilled in the automobile sector and provide hands-on training to them in their fields of interest.
